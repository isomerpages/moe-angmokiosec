<p>&lt;h4&gt;Ang Mo Kio Secondary School Direct School Admission Exercise
2026&lt;/h4&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;Direct School Admission (DSA) is an MOE initiative designed to
give selected</p>
<p>schools greater flexibility in student admission, so that a more diverse</p>
<p>range of student achievements and talents can be recognised.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;In April 2008, MOE approved the school’s application for niche
development</p>
<p>in the area of Symphonic Band. Since then, Ang Mo Kio Secondary School</p>
<p>(AMKSS) has been offering discretionary admission to talented band students</p>
<p>who displayed the passion for band music. With the introduction of the</p>
<p>Learning for Life Programme (LLP) in Arts Education, AMKSS has offered</p>
<p>DSA places for a wider range of performing arts from 2017. From 2019,
we</p>
<p>have broadened our DSA offerings to include the area of Sports as well.</p>
<p>Up to 20% of the Secondary One vacancies will be offered for DSA places</p>
<p>for&amp;nbsp;&lt;strong&gt;Concert Band, Choir, Modern Dance, Malay Dance,
English Drama, Badminton, Basketball, Football, Netball and Volleyball&lt;/strong&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;In line with the school's belief to provide a balanced and holistic
education,</p>
<p>the Co-Curricular Programmes have been designed to allow our students
to</p>
<p>experience an enriching learning journey over the four years in school,</p>
<p>and to challenge each member to attain greater heights in their chosen</p>
<p>interest/talent area in performing arts or sports.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;h4&gt;Concert Band&lt;/h4&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;Ang Mo Kio Secondary School Concert Band was first founded in
the 1980s</p>
<p>as a military band. It was converted to a symphonic band in 2001 and has</p>
<p>a current strength of over 100 members. The Band has achieved Gold Awards</p>
<p>and Certificates of Distinction for consecutive SYF Arts Presentation
since</p>
<p>2005 and is currently led by conductors Mr Daniel Kiang and Mr Desmond</p>
<p>Chow.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;Over the years, the band has enthralled the public with different
genres</p>
<p>of music catering to different audiences at various performance venues</p>
<p>such as Esplanade Concert Hall and Victoria Concert Hall, as well as Singapore</p>
<p>Botanic Gardens. In 2018, the Band collaborated with NParks to bring arts</p>
<p>to the community by performing at the Gardens by the Bay for the Singapore</p>
<p>Garden Festival and also at Artists’ Night Out at Dhoby Ghaut Green during</p>
<p>the Singapore Night Festival. In addition, the Band had the privilege
to</p>
<p>perform at Istana during the National Day Open House in 2017 as well as</p>
<p>participated in various SYF events, such as the Parade of Bands (2016)</p>
<p>and SYF Celebrations - Festival Concert at Esplanade Theatre (2019). In</p>
<p>2022 and 2023, the Band had the opportunity to give back to the surrounding</p>
<p>community through their performances at the National Day Observance Ceremony</p>
<p>at Kebun Baru Community Club.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;This year, as part of Esplanade’s Limelight 2024 series, the
AMKSS Concert</p>
<p>Band was invited to perform at the Esplanade Concert Hall. This series</p>
<p>is part of Esplanade’s efforts to recognise some of the best symphonic</p>
<p>bands in Singapore. In addition, the Band was also selected to participate</p>
<p>alongside several other secondary schools and the Singapore Armed Forces</p>
<p>Band in the upcoming National Day Parade 2024.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;Our comprehensive band programme aims to instil a passion for
music-making</p>
<p>in every member and inculcate lifelong learning in music appreciation.</p>
<p>Besides acquiring the skills of playing an instrument, all band members</p>
<p>will undergo music theory lessons such that they complement the learning</p>
<p>and playing of band music. Our programme also seeks to provide band members</p>
<p>with an enriching band experience through concerts, public performances,</p>
<p>band exchanges with foreign school bands and appreciating music in general</p>
<p>to encourage lifelong learning in arts.&lt;/p&gt;</p>

<p>&lt;h4&gt;Choir&lt;/h4&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;The 40-strong versatile Ang Mo Kio Secondary School Choir, comprising</p>
<p>Soprano, Alto, and Baritone is helmed by talented chorister-conductor,</p>
<p>Ms. Cherie Chai who is also a renown A Capella Trainer- Arranger-Producer,</p>
<p>Music Director of professional choirs and 1st professional female vocal</p>
<p>percussionist in Singapore. The choir achieved a Certificate of Distinction</p>
<p>and Certificate of Accomplishment in SYF Arts Presentations in 2019 and</p>
<p>2023 respectively, and participated under Special Consideration in SYF</p>
<p>2021.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;To hone our students’ vocal, personal and public presentation
skills,</p>
<p>the choir enthusiastically volunteers to perform on various public platforms;</p>
<p>including choral exchanges with other schools, community and school events.</p>
<p>Opportunities to upskill are explored through vocal workshops, master
classes</p>
<p>and concerts every year. Theory sessions based on the ABRSM syllabus may</p>
<p>also be carried out for the choir members to better equip them with useful</p>
<p>music knowledge to appreciate the range and breadth of the world music</p>
<p>that they are exposed to.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;The Choir showcases its talents at various school events such
as Awards</p>
<p>Day, AMKsian Showcase and our school’s biennial event, the Night of Music,</p>
<p>Arts and Dance (NOMAD). Prior to the COVID-19 years, the Choir held joint</p>
<p>concert performances at public venues, such as “The Reason We Sing” and</p>
<p>“The Reason We Sing II” with other school choirs as well as the Voices</p>
<p>of Singapore Festival at CHIJmes in 2019.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;Besides singing, the Choir’s Executive Committee, under the guidance
of</p>
<p>the Choir Teachers and Mistress, organises activities to instill, develop</p>
<p>and promote self-discipline, teamwork, confidence and commitment in our</p>
<p>choristers. Every chorister has a responsibility to serve both the Choir</p>
<p>and the community; as led by the Choir's Executive Committee based on
our</p>
<p>philosophy that every AMKsian Chorister "Ever Strives Upwards - Semper</p>
<p>Superne Nitens!"&lt;/p&gt;</p>

<p>&lt;h4&gt;Dance Club&lt;/h4&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;The Dance Club specialises in 2 broad categories of dance, namely&amp;nbsp;&lt;strong&gt;Modern
and Malay Dance&lt;/strong&gt;.</p>
<p>Two external instructors, who are professionally-trained dancers, coach</p>
<p>the two dance groups in the technical and artistic aspects of the dance.</p>
<p>Through our yearly Dance auditions conducted at the start of the year,</p>
<p>we hope to recruit members who are passionate about dance and are committed</p>
<p>to undergo rigorous training to build on their flexibility, physical strength</p>
<p>and stamina.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;The&amp;nbsp;&lt;strong&gt;Modern Dance&lt;/strong&gt;&amp;nbsp;group
strives to nurture and</p>
<p>develop versatile performers. Through dance, students are empowered to</p>
<p>express their emotions and personal stories through movement and rhythm.</p>
<p>The dance group achieved the Certificate of Distinction in 2015, 2019,</p>
<p>2021 and 2023 and the Certificate of Accomplishment in 2017 at the Singapore</p>
<p>Youth Festival (SYF) Arts Presentation. The group also had the honour
of</p>
<p>performing for the SYF Opening Concert at Esplanade in 2016, the Istana</p>
<p>Open House in 2022 and the Kebun Baru National Day Observance Ceremony</p>
<p>in 2023.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;The&amp;nbsp;&lt;strong&gt;Malay Dance&lt;/strong&gt;&amp;nbsp;consists
of an exuberant group</p>
<p>of students with passion in Malay Dance. We provide opportunities for
students</p>
<p>to learn and appreciate Malay Dance and culture. We aim to develop our</p>
<p>students to be graceful dancers and disciplined students. The group achieved</p>
<p>the Certificate of Distinction in 2015, 2017 and 2023 and the Certificate</p>
<p>of Accomplishment in 2019 and 2021 at the SYF Arts Presentation.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;Through our dance programmes such as Schools Dance Exchange and
Collaboration,</p>
<p>we provide our students the opportunities to learn from other instructors</p>
<p>as well as dancers from other schools. These programmes help build confidence</p>
<p>in our students when performing on stage. Besides showcasing their skills</p>
<p>to a variety of audiences, they will also be trained to think critically</p>
<p>about artistic works, relate aesthetically, affectively and cognitively</p>
<p>to the dance art form and the contexts of artistic works as well as explore</p>
<p>and experiment with the art form.&lt;/p&gt;</p>

<p>&lt;h4&gt;Badminton&lt;/h4&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;Badminton – referred to by some as ‘The Fastest Racquet Sport

<p>Badminton places tremendous demands on those who practice the sport.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;Ang Mo Kio Secondary School has made a name for herself in this
tough</p>
<p>but satisfying sport which hones delicate wrist-work skills, nimble footwork</p>
<p>and perseverance. Team AMKSS trains thrice a week in order to help the</p>
<p>students build badminton skills as well as their grit and persistence
in</p>
<p>getting things right.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;Over the years, our team has consistently made our presence felt
in the</p>
<p>South Zone Inter School Badminton Tournament. In 2018, our B Division
Boys</p>
<p>and Girls fought hard to clinch third position. The following year, our</p>
<p>B Division Girls achieved fourth position. In 2021, our B Girls finished</p>
<p>third and in 2022, emerged second in the South Zone Badminton Competition.</p>
<p>This year, the B Division team fought hard and the boys made it into the</p>
<p>quarter-finals of the National School Games (NSG) South Zone Championship.</p>
<p>Against a group of strong opponents, the boys learnt perseverance and
resilience</p>
<p>as they stood their ground and gave their best. The experience was an
enriching</p>
<p>and meaningful one for them.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;Team AMKSS seeks opportunities that enable our players and leaders
to</p>
<p>plan, organise and implement a variety of programmes to benefit both the</p>
<p>school and community. Annually, we organise a primary school badminton</p>
<p>competition, the “AMKSS Invitational”, to promote the sport, encourage</p>
<p>friendly sparring and help raise the standards of the sport amongst primary</p>
<p>school students. In addition, players assist in the coaching of students</p>
<p>in Ang Mo Kio Primary School as part of our partnership and outreach efforts</p>
<p>with our community.&lt;/p&gt;</p>
